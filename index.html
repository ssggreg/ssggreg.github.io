Objective

I am a PhD candidate in Mathematics with a strong focus on AI explainability and data science. My research combines advanced mathematical techniques, machine learning, and interpretability to develop actionable insights in AI-driven decision-making. With expertise in Graph Neural Networks, large language models, and explainability methods, I aim to transition into a data science role in industry where I can apply my skills to solve real-world challenges. I am particularly motivated by the opportunity to make AI systems more interpretable, fair, and impactful, ensuring that businesses and users can trust and effectively leverage machine learning models.

Machine Learning Interpretability Research

PhD in Mathematics (Sep 2020 - Aug 2025) - University of Illinois Chicago

  • Developed methods for generating legal reasoning using large language models (LLMs) through context augmentation and Chain-of-Thought, applied to landlord-tenant disputes. Published in JURIX 2024. Our work was featured in talks at the IDEAL Institute.
  • Designed an interpretability method for Graph Machine Learning models, focusing on counterfactual explanations that provide recourse to users by identifying minimal graph modifications. Under review for ICML 2025.
  • Investigated the mathematical logic foundations underlying Graph ML interpretability, exploring formal reasoning techniques to characterize and improve model explanations. Under review for LICS 2025.
  • Current Projects:
    • Developing a framework for extracting logic-based explanations for Graph Neural Networks (GNNs), leveraging formal logic for interpretability.
    • Using fine-tuned LLMs to generate human-readable explanations for GNN predictions, combining natural language generation with structural graph insights.
